<Section icon={Shield} title="Core Identity: The Speed & Weight System">

Bob’s fighting style, **Freestyle Karate**, is built on a paradox: he possesses the weight of a heavy-body character but the frame data and movement speed of a much lighter fighter. In *Tekken 8*, this identity is emphasized through improved grounded control and mid-range dominance. Mastering **Tekken 8 Bob tips** starts with understanding how to balance these two extremes.

Bob excels at "responding" to the opponent. While his punishment execution can be demanding, his ability to turn a defensive block into a crushing counter-attack is top-tier. His new "Perfect Body" mechanic acts as a versatile attack reversal, allowing him to catch aggressive opponents off-guard.

| Attribute | Profile | Gameplay Impact |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Range** | Mid-Range | Dominates the space just outside of jab range. |
| **Movement** | High Agility | Surprising sidestep and dash speed for his size. |
| **Power** | Heavy Hitter | High raw damage on single-hit counter-hits. |
| **Difficulty** | Intermediate | Requires good timing for just-frames and stances. |

<Section icon={Sparkles} title="Heat System and Advanced Tactics">

Bob's implementation in the *Tekken 8* Heat System is designed to reward precision. Unlike some characters who can simply "mash" during Heat, Bob players should look for specific windows to maximize their advantage.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Heat Activation">
    Enter Heat using a **Heat Engager** from the Spinner Ball stance. This gives Bob a massive frame advantage and allows him to rush down the opponent immediately.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Heat Dash Conversion">
    Use the **Heat Dash** after a landed Engager to extend your combo. In Season 3, Heat Dash scaling has been adjusted, so focus on stable carry rather than just raw damage.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Just-Frame Heat Smash">
    Bob’s **Heat Smash** features a unique just-frame input. If you time the final button press perfectly, Bob deals increased damage and performs an alternate, more cinematic animation.
  </Step>
</Steps>

The timing for the just-frame Heat Smash is crucial for high-level play. Below is a breakdown of the rewards for mastering this timing:

| Heat Smash Type | Damage Multiplier | Visual Effect | Advantage on Block |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Standard** | 1.0x | Basic animation | Minor Advantage |
| **Just-Frame** | 1.2x | Alternate "Perfect" animation | Major Advantage |

<Section icon={Swords} title="Punishment and Combo Fundamentals">

One of the most essential **Tekken 8 Bob tips** is mastering his punishment tools. Bob has a reliable answer for almost every mistake an opponent makes, provided you know the frame data. His 10-frame and 12-frame punishers are among the best in the game for establishing early dominance.

| Frame | Punisher Move | Usage |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **10f** | Jab Series | Standard fast response to stop pressure. |
| **12f** | Giga Jacker | Fast Power Crush to blow through predictable strings. |
| **15f** | Launch Punish | Use his primary launcher for full combo conversion. |
| **While Standing** | WS Punisher | Essential for punishing blocked low attacks. |

When it comes to combos, Bob’s strength is his versatility. He can choose between high-damage "bread and butter" routes or complex wall-carry sequences using Spinner Ball extensions.

**Essential Combo Milestones:**
- **Launch Practice**: Master his standard launcher into a stable aerial carry.
- **Spinner Ball Links**: Practice transitioning from mid-air hits into Spinner Ball to increase carry distance.
- **Wall Enders**: Use Bob's mobility to reposition the opponent against the wall for maximum pressure.

<Callout type="tip" title="Stability over Style">
Bob's large frame can sometimes cause specific combo routes to drop on smaller characters like Xiaoyu. Always prioritize a "stable" combo that works on the entire cast over a "max damage" route that only works on large characters.
</Callout>
