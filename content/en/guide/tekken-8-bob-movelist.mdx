<Section icon={Target} title="Beginner Guide and Combo Progression">

Starting out with Bob requires a solid grasp of spacing and system mechanics before attempting advanced stance-canceling routes. Because Bob is designed as an all-rounder, learning how to switch between simple, high-damage pokes and active stance pressure is the fastest path to victory.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Embrace the Speed & Weight Identity">
    Do not play Bob like a slow, heavy-bodied character. Utilize his surprisingly fast dash and sidestep to bait opponent whiffs, then punish with simple, high-damage strikes.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Establish Close-to-Mid Spacing">
    Keep your engagements within mid-range. Use standard mid strikes like Onigiri Bomber to check incoming movement, and deploy Giga Jacker if the opponent becomes overly aggressive.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Incorporate Universal Systems">
    Get comfortable using Heat Bursts and Heat Engagers early in the round. Activating Heat not only boosts Bob's chip damage but also unlocks his highly rewarding just-frame Heat Smash.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Master Basic Stance Transitions">
    Practice shifting into United Step and Spinner Ball from simple pokes. Focus on maintaining a fluid offensive flow rather than memorizing overly complex, low-probability combo strings.
  </Step>
</Steps>

Once you have mastered the basic movement options and neutral tools, you can begin implementing stable combo routes. Bob's combo structure is highly versatile, allowing for consistent wall carry and high-damage conversions under the current patch rules.

- **Standard Launch Route**: Start with a reliable launcher, transition into a stable mid-air carry string, and finish with a heavy grounded strike to secure solid positioning.
- **Spinner Ball Aerial Extension**: Incorporate Spinner Ball transitions mid-combo to carry opponents across the stage. Use Roulette Cutter to bridge the gap and maximize wall-splat potential.
- **Heat Dash Conversions**: After landing a Heat Engager, trigger a Heat Dash to extend your combo route. Be mindful of Season 3 damage scaling rules when planning these high-resource conversions.
- **Wall Carry and Enders**: Guide your opponent toward the wall using Bob's excellent forward-moving strikes. Once at the wall, apply heavy grounded pressure or United Step mix-ups to close out the round.
